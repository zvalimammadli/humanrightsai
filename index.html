About the Summit

The International Baku Summit of Ombudsmen serves as an international platform for discussing the opportunities, risks and responsibilities arising from the development of artificial intelligence, while promoting approaches grounded in human rights principles.

The Summit brings together Ombudsman institutions, National Human Rights Institutions, international organizations, public authorities, civil society representatives and experts to exchange views on the impact of artificial intelligence on human rights, democratic governance, equality, privacy and access to justice.

Date: 18–19 June 2026
Venue: JW Marriott Absheron Baku
Location: Baku, Azerbaijan

Main Programme

International Baku Summit of Ombudsmen on “Human Rights in the Age of Artificial Intelligence: Opportunities, Risks and Responsibilities”

Plenary Session

Human Rights in the Age of Artificial Intelligence (AI): Opportunities, Risks and Responsibilities

  • The role of artificial intelligence technologies in the promotion and protection of human rights
  • Promotion of a human-centered and rights-based approach to AI
  • Strengthening the monitoring and advisory role of Ombudsman institutions and NHRIs
  • Enhancing international cooperation and developing common standards
Parallel Session I

Protection of Rights in Digital Space: Data Protection, Privacy and Combating Discrimination

  • Protection of personal data and legal safeguards in the era of AI
  • The impact of disinformation on human rights and the administration of justice
  • Balancing digital surveillance mechanisms with fundamental rights and freedoms
  • Protection of human dignity in the digital environment
Parallel Session II

The Impact of AI on the Labor Market and Employment

  • Labor rights and social protection mechanisms in the context of technological advancement
  • Building an inclusive and equitable labor market through a human rights-based approach
  • Changing skills requirements, reskilling and the right to education in the age of AI
  • Legal safeguards in the digital economy and non-standard forms of employment
Parallel Session III

Transparency, Justice and Equality in AI Systems

  • The impact of automated decision-making on human rights
  • Ethical standards and legal frameworks governing AI technologies
  • Data bias and its impact on social equality
  • Prevention of discrimination risks in AI systems
Parallel Session IV

Digital Literacy and Protection of Human Rights

  • Protection of rights and development of safe digital skills
  • Awareness-raising in the context of digital transformation
  • Digital security for vulnerable population groups
  • State policies, programmes and strategies in this field
Closing Session

Adoption of the Baku Declaration on AI and Human Rights

Baku Declaration

The Baku Declaration on Artificial Intelligence and Human Rights will reflect the key outcomes of the International Baku Summit of Ombudsmen.

The document will be made available after its adoption by the participants of the Summit.
