About the Summit

A global dialogue on technology and human dignity

The Baku Ombudsmen Summit 2026 is dedicated to one of the most important questions of our time: how can artificial intelligence serve human beings while fully respecting human rights, dignity, equality and the rule of law?


Artificial intelligence is already transforming public administration, education, healthcare, employment, security, communication, justice and urban development. These changes create new opportunities, but they also raise serious questions about privacy, discrimination, transparency, access to remedies and accountability.


In this context, Ombudsmen and National Human Rights Institutions have a special role. They can monitor the human rights impact of new technologies, advise public authorities, engage with civil society, receive complaints, promote awareness and contribute to responsible governance of AI systems.

Why AI and Human Rights?

Technology must remain human-centred

Artificial intelligence can improve access to services, support decision-making, strengthen education, assist persons with disabilities, improve public management and help societies respond to complex challenges. At the same time, if AI systems are designed or used without safeguards, they may deepen inequality, limit privacy, reproduce discrimination, reduce transparency and affect access to justice.

Opportunity

AI can help institutions provide faster, more inclusive and more accessible services for people.

Risk

AI can create risks for privacy, equality, non-discrimination, freedom of expression and due process.

Responsibility

Public authorities, companies and institutions must ensure that AI is used in a transparent, accountable and rights-based manner.

Role of Ombudsmen and NHRIs

Human rights institutions in the digital age

Ombudsmen and National Human Rights Institutions are bridges between people, public authorities and international human rights standards. In the age of AI, this role becomes even more important. They can help ensure that digital transformation does not leave anyone behind.

Monitoring

Assessing the impact of AI systems on human rights, especially where public services and vulnerable groups are concerned.

Advising

Providing recommendations to state bodies on laws, policies and safeguards related to artificial intelligence.

Complaints and Remedies

Supporting access to effective remedies where people are affected by automated or technology-assisted decisions.

Awareness

Promoting digital literacy, human rights education and public understanding of AI-related risks and opportunities.

Main Themes

Key topics for discussion

The Summit will focus on the practical human rights implications of artificial intelligence and digital transformation.

Human-Centred AI

Ensuring that AI systems are designed and used in a way that protects human dignity, autonomy and fundamental rights.

Privacy and Personal Data

Addressing the protection of personal data, responsible data governance and the prevention of intrusive surveillance.

Equality and Non-Discrimination

Preventing algorithmic bias and ensuring that AI systems do not reproduce or deepen existing inequalities.

Children in the Digital Era

Protecting children’s rights, safety, privacy and development in online and AI-powered environments.

Transparency and Accountability

Promoting explainable, accountable and reviewable AI systems, especially in public decision-making.

Access to Justice

Ensuring effective remedies when people are affected by automated or technology-assisted decisions.

Baku as a Dialogue Platform

A meeting point for international cooperation

Baku has increasingly become a platform for international dialogue on human rights, cooperation and global challenges. The Baku Ombudsmen Summit continues this tradition by bringing together institutions and experts to discuss one of the most urgent issues of the digital era.


The Summit will also highlight the importance of knowledge, culture and human creativity. The planned cultural programme to Gobustan Rock Art Cultural Landscape symbolically connects the earliest forms of human expression with the digital code shaping our future.

From Stone to Code

The story of human progress continues

From the first human marks on stone to the digital code shaping our modern world, humanity’s journey has always been a journey of knowledge, creativity and responsibility.

From stone to science. From symbols to technology. From human expression to artificial intelligence. The question remains the same: will technology shape humanity, or will humanity shape technology?

The answer requires cooperation, knowledge, responsibility and a strong commitment to human rights.
